## Ingredients
Chicken Marinade:
- 2 cup full-fat yogurt
- 4 teaspoons salt
- 2.5 tablespoons garam masala
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 3 tablespoon mince garlic
- 4 inch squeeze of mince ginger- from tube
- 2 kg chicken thighs, cut into 1” pieces

Sauce:
- 3 Tbsp vegetable oil
- 2 large yellow onion, thinly sliced
- 4 inch squeeze of mince garlic - from tube
- 2 Tbsp mince garlic
- 2 tsp paprika
- 2 tsp) cayenne powder
- 2 Tbsp garam masala
- 2 Tbsp unsalted butter
- 2x 425g can of crushed tomatoes
- 2 cup heavy cream
- salt to taste

## Method

Chicken:
1. In a medium-sized bowl, add yogurt, salt, garam masala, paprika, garlic, and ginger; combine.
2. Add chicken to your bowl, toss to coat thoroughly, and marinate for 10 minutes - overnight in the fridge.

Sauce:
1. **Optional**. Set a 10-inch pan over medium-high heat, and add enough vegetable oil to coat the bottom of the pan.

Once hot and nearly smoking, add all your chicken in batches and sear for 2 - 3 minutes per side until nicely browned on all edges.

1. Remove chicken, add additional oil if needed, and lower the heat to medium.
2. Add onion, ginger, and garlic. Season lightly with salt, and saute for 3 - 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
3. Next, add paprika, cayenne, garam masala; saute until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
4. Add crushed tomatoes, stir and reduce for 5 minutes.
5. **Optional**. Pour into a blender and blend on high speed until as smooth as possible. Place back in the pot,
6. reduce the heat to low, add all your chicken, and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes or until the chicken is thoroughly cooked and the sauce is reduced.
7. Stir in heavy cream and reduce for 7 - 8 more minutes or until the sauce is thick and velvety—adjust salt levels if necessary.
8. Serve immediately with steamed white rice. Optionally garnish with cilantro.
